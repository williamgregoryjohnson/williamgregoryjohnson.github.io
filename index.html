I am a PhD candidate and Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science, Georgia State University.

CV, BIO

Currently, I am under advisement with Dr. Anu G. Bourgeois.

In 2004, I completed my Masters of Science in Computer Science at Georgia State University Department of Computer Science under the supervision of Dr. Sushil Prasad.

My research interests include computer science education with undergraduate students and centered around performance prediction and classification using data mining techniques. By solely considering pedagogical approaches, students are all being treated the same, regardless of their individual needs. Using combined student features like race, sex, transfer status, prerequisite grades, CS course grades, family indicators, and behaviors in course selection or sequencing, knowledge discovery is made to improve CS sub-group educational experiences and outcomes. My discoveries aid in developing personalized interventions towards program retention, faster pathways in four-year degree completion and higher overall performance.
