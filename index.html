I am a PhD candidate and Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science, Georgia State University.

CV, BIO

Currently, I am under advisement with Dr. Anu G. Bourgeois.

In 2004, I completed my Masters of Science in Computer Science at Georgia State University Department of Computer Science under the supervision of Dr. Sushil Prasad.

My research interests are CS education with undergraduate students, namely, performance prediction and classification. By solely considering pedagogical approaches, students are all being treated the same, regardless of their individual needs. I instead look at combined features like race, sex, transfer status, prerequisite grades, CS course grades, family indicators, and behaviors in course selection or sequencing. This opens ways of knowledge discovery to improve numerous CS sub-group educational experiences and outcomes. My discoveries lead to developing personalized interventions (based upon demographics, behaviors, and other combined features) to position students towards program retention, pathways towards 4yr degree completion and higher overall performance.
