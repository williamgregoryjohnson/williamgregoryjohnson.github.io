I am a PhD candidate and Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science, Georgia State University.

CV, BIO

Currently, I am under advisement with Dr. Anu G. Bourgeois.

In 2004, I completed my Masters of Science in Computer Science at Georgia State University Department of Computer Science under the supervision of Dr. Sushil Prasad.

My research interests are in Computer Science Education Research for undergraduate students. By solely considering pedagogical approaches, students are all being treated the same, regardless of their individual needs. Instead of focusing on pedagogy and content delivery, we can instead look at student factors like race, sex, transfer status, prerequisite grades, course grades, and behaviors in course selection and sequencing to identify ways of improving performance, educational experiences, and outcomes. My discoveries can lead to developing customized interventions (based on student demographics and behaviors) to position students on pathways towards success. I am a Fellow in the Neuroscience Institute, Brains and Behavior at GSU. Also, I am a Fellow in the Neuroscience Institute, Brains and Behavior at GSU.
